About FPTV Skills Challenge 2026

Showcasing TVET excellence and preparing students for the workforce of tomorrow.

About the Event

The FPTV Skills Challenge (FPTVSC) is proudly organized by the Faculty of Technical and Vocational Education (FPTV), Universiti Tun Hussein Onn Malaysia, in conjunction with Hari TVET Negara — a national initiative dedicated to celebrating and strengthening Malaysia’s Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) ecosystem.

By aligning with Hari TVET Negara, FPTVSC underscores its role as more than a student competition: it is a national movement to elevate skills, foster innovation, and connect academia with industry demands.

Mission & Vision

Mission
  • Enhance TVET graduates’ competitiveness through real-world skill challenges.
  • Promote industry-aligned competencies reflecting workplace demands.
  • Foster creativity, innovation, and collaboration among students.
  • Establish FPTVSC as a benchmark competition aligned with national and international TVET agendas.
Vision
  • Be recognized as Malaysia’s leading TVET skills competition.
  • Expand to MTUN and ASEAN participation.
  • Support Hari TVET Negara and Malaysia’s graduate employability goals.
  • Champion innovation and excellence in TVET education.

Growth & Impact

  • Started in 2024: Hosted by FPTV, UTHM, with sponsors steadily growing alongside the event.
  • Rising Participation: Students compete in pre-qualification rounds, with only the best reaching the finals.
  • Expanding Reach: While currently within FPTV, FPTVSC is set to expand across MTUN and beyond.
  • Industry Relevance: Endorsed by industry experts, ensuring challenges mirror workplace demands.

Our Journey

2024 – The Beginning

FPTV Skills Challenge started at UTHM with participation limited to FPTV students.

2025 – Growing Recognition

More sponsors came onboard as the competition gained attention for its TVET focus.

2026 – Towards Expansion

Pre-qualification rounds introduced, with participants expanding beyond FPTV into other faculties within UTHM, strengthening collaboration across disciplines.

Future – MTUN Collaboration

Exploring integration with the Malaysian Technical University Network (MTUN), a strategic alliance of four universities (UTHM, UTeM, UMP, UniMAP). This collaboration aims to elevate TVET competitions into a broader platform, preparing the ground for eventual national and ASEAN-level showcases.
